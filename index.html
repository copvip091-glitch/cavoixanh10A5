🐋 BLUE WHALE

Introduction

The blue whale (Balaenoptera musculus) is the largest animal that has ever lived on Earth. It can grow up to 30 meters long and weigh more than 180 tons.

Images and Information About Blue Whales

Blue Whale

Blue whales are the largest animals that have ever lived on Earth. They can reach about 25–30 meters in length and weigh between 100–180 tons. Their bodies are blue-gray with light spots.

Blue Whale Feeding

The main food of blue whales is krill (a small shrimp-like animal). An adult blue whale can eat about 3–4 tons of krill per day during the feeding season.

Mother and Calf

Blue whales are mammals, so they give birth and feed their babies with milk. The pregnancy lasts about 10–12 months. A newborn whale is about 7 meters long and weighs around 2–3 tons.

Whale Hunting

In the past, blue whales were heavily hunted for their oil and meat. This greatly reduced their population and made them an endangered species.

Whale Skeleton

Blue whale skeletons are extremely large and are often displayed in natural history museums. They help people understand the enormous size of this animal.

⚠ Risk of Extinction

  • Whale hunting in the past
  • Ocean pollution
  • Climate change
  • Ship collisions
  • Underwater noise pollution
